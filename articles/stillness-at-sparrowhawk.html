Stillness at Sparrowhawk

by Bruno Grossi

Aug 16, 2024

I felt like I had discovered the taste of tranquility there. Sparrowhawk at Spray Lake. I can picture the image of the moment in my mind, where the sudden chill and pass of the feeling left its calling card. Staring out at the view of the nature surrounding Sparrowhawk at Spray Lake.

It was the missing movement of the breeze, the stalled air above the hill we stood on was cool. Little weeds grew in-between the rocky surface, sprouting their leaves out the silty gray pastiche, curving downwards to the rocky shore. I saw the lone fisherman, small and still, staring out, his rod as unmoving as he was, imitating the waters he grazed. Spray Lake expanded like a mirror to the sky. A reflection so perfect the glare would stare back at you for watching too long. The lake continued to extend in subtle curves around the peninsulas of avalanche that slipped into the edges. The mountains would dip their toes in this way. Crawling just a little further in during the sweltering summer, they’d ease back in the relief of the glacial water. The trousers of deep green hiking up their sides, thinning out to expose the defining stone, carved sharp, the couture of shadows curtain across the side of their faces. The pockets of darkness littering Goat Mountain look like soft craters, refuge from the blistering July heat. The white hot sun is relentless and pours through the patchwork of leaves, the blips of dimmed light are scattered along the path leading down the hill from the forest. I watched the forest from the other side, imagining this same littering of light through the flora appearing all around at a moment like this.

My uncle crouched at the rocky shore down the north face of the hill we stood on. He’d taken his shoes off to step in the water. Like the mountains, he too eased his body with the coldness of the water surrounding him. I watched him slip into this peace through the growth of the brush. He dropped his hands into the blue, submerging his fingers and then submitting his wrists after a moment of breath. A pulse of waves extended from the stem where he laid his arms. I watched it extend to the white shoreline, curving north like a lazy goose. The shoreline turned sharp, disappearing behind itself, and my gaze was left with the blue fabric of the water again. Continuously crawling on and on until it met the seam of the next shore further down and exploding into the monumentally consuming body of the mountain. All covered again with the deep forest and textured shale and silt where it once gave out, spilling itself into Spray Lake, giving it another curve, another shore. My vision returns to my uncle, still dipping his arms into the lake’s glacial refuge, breathing out slowly the summer heat, appearing only as a dot next to the immensity of the unmoving landscape.

It is these captured moments of nigh unmoving scenery at Sparrowhawk that are granted the title of Ideal. Scanning this landscape, not merely investigating, but admiring the subtleties of the flora and endless ridges seemingly sealed in time finally delivered the pang of true natural tranquility to my heart. Even though there was movement - the insects that buzz and bite around me and under the dirt I stand, the myriad of animals hidden among the trees along the shores and the unknown invisible beings living in the waters of Spray Lake - the feeling I was placed into a painted scenery was unpassable. Time had caught up to the sensation and arrived at serenity. Nothing that wasn’t quiet dripped away to silence. I became static like the world, simply becoming part of it. I could see the fisherman, still just as he was, and my uncle too, both so distant, and still matching the part. I felt my heart’s suspension from its strings slowly unravel while my eyes followed the line of the sky and the stone. It unwrapped and spun in my body, freefalling past my organs and landing in the bottom of something. Tears began to well, blurring my vision of the beauty bestowed. My face contorted into nothing, only the tears summoned any courage in the moment to break the stillness, leaving a salty trail down the curve of my face, before falling to the dusty ground and evaporating in an instant on the stones. The peace of the moment was not disturbed by this and so the tears continued to follow the path set out. Just as the mountains carved out a trail for the winter’s snow to fill the basin, these tears too followed a familiar path. So suddenly my muscles froze and my eyes were drowned in the full beauty of the land before me.


Hours later, sitting at an overcrowded restaurant table, the heat of the long day slowly baking my brain through the oven of my skull, I stared dismayed at the food. Though I had ordered what had sounded good to me, a dish I cannot even remember the name of now, my body feigned fullness at the seemingly hostile air. The atmosphere breathed loudly. Innumerable voices, agreeing that “absolutely, we saw some very beautiful places today”. A sentiment that seemed to bounce back and forth in a volley. Unbelievable buzz happening all around me that was a spinning string, swinging around my neck, graceful pressure that built and built to a rope’s suffocation.

I got up in a way that felt like that rope had yanked on me. The vacant bathroom offered a short respite. Sitting there in the private stall, eyes fixed on the bottom of the door handle, listening to the low rumble of patronage and footsteps upstairs, I began to see the shadows of the mountains at Sparrowhawk in the floats of my vision. The unmoving reflections of Spray Lake just beneath came back to me. I focused my thoughts on the stillness at Sparrowhawk, everything restful and away from this place. Staring at what was actually the corner of the bathroom floor, I felt the soft breeze and watched how the low branches swayed like a slow ballet. Their moving shadows that would bring and take the warmth of the sun with their rhythm. I felt like I could still turn to look down the hill and see my uncle with his hands in the cold of the water. Waving his wrists just below the surface to cool down from the day’s heat. His shoes are sitting on the shale leading to the lake. The day is so clear the water was a reflection of the sky. So the curtain of quiet enraptured me and I heard nothing of the chaos and panic I had escaped. Instead, I started out as far as I could, to where the lake begins to curve behind the mountains. All I thought was for when I could return there.
